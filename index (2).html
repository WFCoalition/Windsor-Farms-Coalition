How to Use RVA 311

  1. Download the RVA311 app or go to rva311.com
  2. Create an account / log in
  3. Select "Create New Request"
  4. Choose the appropriate category
  5. Add location details and upload photos
  6. Submit — you'll receive email updates on status
  7. Search the map for existing requests and upvote them to increase prioritization

Issue No. 3 — May 2026

The Windsor Farms Julep

There are few drinks more closely tied to Virginia than the Mint Julep. Long before it became synonymous with Churchill Downs, the julep was a staple across the Tidewater and Piedmont. This version stays true to that tradition with a subtle Virginia touch: local honey in place of sugar.

  • 2 oz bourbon
  • ¼ oz Virginia honey syrup (equal parts honey and warm water)
  • 6–8 fresh mint leaves

Gently press the mint with the honey syrup to release its oils. Add the bourbon, then fill the cup with crushed ice and stir until the exterior frosts. Top with additional ice and garnish with a fresh mint bouquet.

The key is restraint. Best enjoyed outdoors, at the end of the day, with no particular urgency to be anywhere else.

Issue No. 2 — March 2026

The Windsor Farms Rickey

Born in Washington, D.C. in the late nineteenth century, the original Rickey was a drink of clarity — gin, lime, and soda. This variation keeps that spirit intact while allowing for the quiet arrival of spring.

  • 2 oz gin
  • ½ fresh lime, squeezed and dropped in
  • 2 thin slices cucumber
  • 2–3 mint leaves, lightly expressed
  • Ice & soda water to top

Combine in a tall glass. Add ice, top with soda water, and stir once, briefly. No sugar. No additional garnish.

Best taken in the early evening, when the light is still present but beginning to fade.

Issue No. 1 — March 2026

The Black Swan Boulevardier

Born in the 1920s and favored in both Paris and New York drawing rooms, the Boulevardier offers a balance of warmth and sophistication.

  • 2 oz bourbon
  • 1 oz sweet vermouth
  • 1 oz Campari
  • Orange peel

Stir with ice until well chilled. Strain into a chilled coupe or small stemmed glass. Express an orange peel over the top and discard.

For a softer finish, add ¼ oz Tuaca before stirring.
